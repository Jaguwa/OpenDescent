May 8: the day Meta admitted it wants to read your DMs.

Meta said it is removing Instagram's end-to-end encryption because "very few people were opting in." That's true in the sense that "very few people use the emergency exit on a train" — not because they don't want to, but because it was buried, hard to find, and off by default. A closer look at what Meta is actually doing, and why the story isn't about Instagram at all.

Mandatory age verification is a privacy disaster by design.

Discord leaked 70,000 government IDs in 2025. Its verification partner Persona exposed another 2,500 files in 2026. This isn't about Discord's implementation. The moment any company is responsible for holding 100 million driver's licenses, that company becomes the most valuable target on the internet — and the breach is eventually inevitable.
